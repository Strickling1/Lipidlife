Myth busters
Common myth
"I feel fine, so my cholesterol can't be dangerous."
High LDL has no symptoms. Arterial plaque builds silently for years before a heart attack. The first symptom is sometimes the event itself.
Common myth
"Eating cholesterol raises my blood cholesterol."
Dietary cholesterol (eggs, shrimp) has minimal impact for most people. Saturated and trans fats are the main driver of LDL — not food cholesterol itself.
Common myth
"Once on statins, I don't need to change my diet."
Statins reduce LDL by 30–50%, but lifestyle adds another 20–30% — and improves HDL and triglycerides, which statins do less for.
Gulf context
"Shisha is safer than cigarettes for my heart."
One hour of shisha delivers more carbon monoxide than a full pack of cigarettes. The cardiovascular harm is equivalent to heavy smoking.

Ramadan guidance
Eating for your heart during Ramadan
Break fast with 2–3 dates + water — ideal. Avoid heavy fried foods at Iftar. The spike in saturated fat after a long fast is absorbed more aggressively. Opt for lentil soup, grilled fish, and whole grains. Suhoor: oats with nuts keeps cholesterol low through the fast.
